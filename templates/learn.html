What is the Offside Rule?

If you’ve ever watched a football match, you’ve probably heard the term “offside” thrown around...

Why does the rule exist?

It prevents players from just waiting near the goal for an easy score. It's meant to keep the game fair and strategic.

A player is offside if they’re closer to the opponent’s goal than the ball and second-last defender when the ball is played.

Offside depends on your position and timing. It's about when the ball is passed — not when it's received.

You can't be offside in your own half!

You’re also not offside if you’re level with the ball or second-last defender.

And you're not offside if you're not actively involved in the play.
